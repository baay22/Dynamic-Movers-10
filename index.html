What movers cannot pack for you?

Despite the detailed services that professional movers render, there are still certain things they will not do for you. This includes cash, jewelry, and food items – whether raw or frozen – it is best to transport such items in your vehicle.

Many professional movers will also not move items like chemicals, pesticides, gasoline, live plants, animals, large equipment, and ammunition. You might also need to find alternative ways of moving items like scuba tanks, batteries, and other prohibited items.

Before a moving company agrees to offer you their services, they will tell you the conditions and you consent to them. Generally, the prohibition is to protect certain sensitive belongings from theft or getting missing during packing and unpacking. Also, very personal belongings are best left in the care of the owners. Ensure you get a comprehensive list of prohibited items from your movers before your pay for their services.

Cost of using Professional Movers

Generally, the cost of hiring movers varies from state to state, municipal to municipal. The services you require also determine the amount they charge you. While many movers charge hourly rates, some have comprehensive charges to cover the full services they render to you.

Some of the factors that may be considered before payment is finalised are done movers pack for you? How many movers do you need? How many properties or belongings do you have to move? What is the distance between the old and new residence? What types of packing materials do you require? And many others.

For full-service moving companies, you can budget between $40 – $60 per hour. The number of men required moving, the type of apartment you’re moving from and the size of your load, as well as the period of the year you decide to move, may all affect the pricing.

Just as you would normally do when hiring any other services, it is good to get estimates from two to three different movers and check which is better. Never forget to check their list of services and other terms and conditions, so you know exactly what you are paying for.
